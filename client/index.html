    Harpooning An Auction

    The "harpoon" button offers any bidder in an auction a once-per-auction chance to steal the pot from the current active bidder. More recently-placed bids have a higher chance of successfully stealing the pot. The odds of a harpoon succeeding are displayed on the harpoon button as a percentage likelihood of success. When the harpoon button is pressed, a provably-fair random number is calculated, and the bidder's current harpooning chances are compared against this random number. If the harpoon succeeds, the auction is over and the entire pot is awarded to the player who pressed the harpoon button. If the harpoon fails, the player who pressed the harpoon button can no longer participate in that specific auction, although other bidders in the auction can bid or to use their own harpoon buttons.

    • You must be a participating bidder in an auction in order to harpoon.
    • A bidder is not allowed to harpoon if he/she is the current leader.
    • You cannot harpoon this auction until a minimum of bids have been placed.
    • Your odds of successfully harpooning are dependent on how many other bidders have bid more recently than you. Your odds are published on the harpoon button and change as new bidders join an auction.
    • If you are the leader in an auction and all other participating bidders in that auction harpoon you, you stand a chance of surviving all harpoons.
    Provably Fair
    Your Player Seed:
    Auto-randomize

    When you click the "harpoon" button and try to steal the pot, we compute a random number to determine whether or not the harpoon will be successful. The random number is generated using a "house seed" that is selected by EOSTime and a "player seed" that is determined by the person who is in jeopardy of losing the pot (the player who is the current active bidder). A bidder's player seed is sent to our servers each time a bid is placed.

    This means that the random number can be proven to be fair, in that it requires seeds from both EOSTime (the "house") and the leading bidder. We do not disclose the house's seed before the random number is generated, but we do disclose its SHA-256 hash, which can be used later to prove fairness, as described below.

    Your player seed sent to us each time you bid can be randomly generated by your browser on each bid, or explicitely set by you using the form at the top. Your player seed must be an integer <= 100000.

    The random number is between 0 and 4294967295 (2^32) (inclusive), and is computed by concatenating the player seed and the house seed, then taking SHA-512 hash of that combination, and finally using the first 32 bits (8 hex digits) of this hash as the 32-bit random number.

    Let's say for example, when an auction is first started, we select a server seed of 072b1fc2-d121-d9cb-2fd4-3fa902a12677. We do not disclose this seed, but do publish its SHA-256 hash at the bottom of the displayed auction:

    43201cdad9dd01e5d6cdaaf51adda76f0f999b69c1b23150b80ab893a1598b3c

    Let's assume the player seed of the leading bidder was 79561. When the harpoon button is pressed, our server generates a random number by taking the leading bidder's player seed (sent when the leader bid was placed) and concatenating the undisclosed house seed to yield the string:

    79561072b1fc2-d121-d9cb-2fd4-3fa902a12677

    We then take the SHA-512 hash of this value, yielding (in hexadecimal)

    d4282b56805ca1bdc140ea5b854c7c87d05078fa86bdb0183cf62336f9ddb16d579da9c669ba847d5f1af8896a75aeb087b0a3844567a880a8962299c6c8b5e0

    The first 32 bits of this value are represented by the first eight hex digits, in this case, 0xD4282B56. Converting this to a decimal integer will yield a random number between 0 and 4294967295 (inclusive), or, in this example, 3559402326.

    In order for the player's harpoon to succeed, the random number must be less than or equal to the odds published on the harpoon button when pressed multiplied by the maximum range of the random number (4294967295):

    Random # must be <= Math.round(odds * 4294967295)

    After the harpoon has been processed, we publish the oringal server seed which hashes to the value published on the auction.

    Because the player seed (79561) was chosen by the bidder who is being harpooned and displayed on the auction along with the server seed hash prior to the harpoon button being pressed, the harpoon's randomness is provably fair. All players can prove that neither the house nor any player was able to individually affect the resulting random number.
